# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

GLIMPS welcomes contributors and users of every background and experience
level. Participation must remain respectful, constructive, and safe.

Examples of welcome behavior include:

- discussing technical tradeoffs without attacking people;
- giving specific, actionable feedback;
- respecting different experience levels and communication styles;
- crediting other people's work; and
- protecting private or sensitive information encountered while debugging.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized
attention, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption, publishing private
information, or retaliating against someone who reports a concern.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, release and
support channels, and public spaces where someone is representing GLIMPS.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, commits, or
other material that violates this code. Responses may include a warning,
temporary restriction, or permanent removal from project spaces, depending on
severity and pattern of behavior.

Report conduct concerns privately through GitHub's **Security → Report a
vulnerability** form and identify the report as a code-of-conduct matter. If the
private form is unavailable, request private maintainer contact in a public
issue without naming people or including incident details.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. Maintainers
with a conflict of interest must not participate in deciding the response.

## Good-Faith Participation

Disagreement, rejected contributions, and respectful criticism are not conduct
violations. Knowingly false reports, harassment through the reporting process,
or attempts to expose another person's private information are violations.
